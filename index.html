About me

Welcome to my website!

My name is Daniel, a PhD-student affiliated with the National University of Singapore (NUS) and Genome Institute of Singapore (GIS). I'm fascinated by chromosomes and the interplay between genetic and epigenetic factors during cancer progression. My projects include: 1) The profiling of copy number and histone modification heterogeneity in primary and progressed head and neck cancer (HNSC) and 2) Investigating the role of (extrachromosomal) YAP1 amplification in mediating HNSC progression through novel cis- and trans-interactions. As a hybrid scientist, I use both dry- and wetlab techniques in these projects. Please see my research page for more details!

A nomad originally from Indonesia, I've also lived in the Netherlands (~16y) and the UK (~2y) before coming to Singapore. Besides doing research, I enjoy reading the Bible, going for walks, and culture trips.
